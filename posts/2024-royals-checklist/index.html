As a Kansas City Royals fan, the 2014-2015 seasons feel like well a decade ago. And unfortunately it will be a decade since this baseball team felt relevant and I think they would agree with me. Today I want to look at where the Royals problems lie (which are a lot), and try and point out why they lost over 100 games.

The Atlanta Braves, who the Royals tried modeling their system after, due to former General Manager Dayton Moore coming from Atlanta, lead the major leagues in about every offensive category this last season. They truly were the greatest offense on paper in MLB history in 2023. But how bad was the Royals offensive numbers?

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggrepel)
library(cowplot)

logs <- read_csv("teamhittingwarmlb.csv")

top10 <- logs %>% top_n(10, wt=WAR)
bottom10 <- logs %>% top_n(10, wt=-WAR)

kc <- bottom10 %>% filter(Tm == "Kansas City Royals")
mn <- top10 %>% filter(Tm == "Minnesota Twins")

bar1 <- ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=top10, aes(x=reorder(Tm, WAR), weight=WAR)) +
  geom_bar(data=mn, aes(x=reorder(Tm, WAR), weight=WAR), fill="#D31145") +
  coord_flip() + 
  labs(title="Best & Worst offensive WAR totals in MLB", subtitle = "The Royals find themselves in the bottom per usual, with BWJ accounting for 4.4 of it.. ",  x="Team", y="WAR", caption = "") + 
  theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank()
    )

bar2 <- ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=bottom10, aes(x=reorder(Tm, -WAR), weight=WAR)) +

  coord_flip() +  
  labs(title = "", subtitle= "", x="", y="",  caption="Source: Pro Baseball Reference | By Jackson Reddick") + 
  theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8),

    )

plot_grid(bar1, bar2)

So the Royals found themselves in the bottom ten of Wins Above Replacement on the offensive side of the ball. People will debate that WAR doesn’t mean all, but frankly it shows how valuable the players on your team are, and the Royals didn’t have that many valuable players.

But in baseball you have to pitch as well. Around the trade deadline the Royals made a trade for Cole Ragans, a great young LHP, who looks to be a staple of the Royals pitching staff for years to come. Everyone else, well maybe not so much. The most basic stats in baseball are important from the pitching side, walks and strikeouts. Allowing free bases and the lack of striking batters out will equate to bad baseball.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggrepel)

logs <- read_csv("2023mlbsp.csv")

pitching <- logs |> 
  group_by(Tm) |> 
  summarise(
    TotalWalks = sum(BB),
    TotalStrikeouts = sum(SO)
  )

alcentral <- pitching |> 
  filter(Tm == "Kansas City Royals" | Tm == "Detroit Tigers" | Tm == "Cleveland Guardians" | Tm == "Chicago White Sox" | Tm == "Minnesota Twins")

ggplot() + 
  geom_point(
    data=pitching, aes(x=TotalStrikeouts, y=TotalWalks), color="grey", alpha=.5
    ) +
  geom_point(
    data=alcentral, aes(x=TotalStrikeouts, y=TotalWalks), color="blue"
    ) +
  geom_vline(xintercept = 1394.767) +
  geom_hline(yintercept = 527.3) +
    geom_text_repel(
    data=alcentral, 
    aes(x=TotalStrikeouts, y=TotalWalks, label=Tm)
  ) +
  geom_text(aes(x=1500, y=700, label="Good K% Bad BB%"), color="red") +
  geom_text(aes(x=1500, y=400, label="Elite K/BB %"), color="red") +
  geom_text(aes(x=1200, y=700, label="Bad K/BB %"), color="red") +
  geom_text(aes(x=1200, y=400, label="Bad K% Good BB%"), color="red") +
  labs(title="Where do the Royals basic pitching stats lie?", subtitle="Royals pitchers didn't lead the league the walks and sure didn't in K's", caption="Source: Pro Baseball Reference | By Jackson Reddick")  + theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank()
    )

So the Royals pitchers were in the one quadrant you don’t want to find yourself in. They weren’t among the top five in walks, but were in the bottom five of strikeouts in the MLB.

The Royals are in a weird situation where they don’t know what stadium they want to play in, I am still confused on where they are going to play baseball in ten years time. Nonetheless, Kauffman Stadium is a pitcher’s ballpark, maybe the most pitcher friendly ballpark in the MLB. The outfield is huge, and lots of home runs turn into outs at Kauffman. But how did the Royals stack up in home ballpark adjusted stats?

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggalt)
library(ggrepel)

mlb <- read_csv("2023mlbbatterrankings.csv")

royals <- mlb |> filter(Tm == "KCR")

royalsPA <- royals |> 
  filter(PA > 135)

melendez <- royalsPA |> filter(Name == "MJ Melendez")
massey <- royalsPA |> filter(Name == "Michael Massey")

ggplot() +
  geom_lollipop(
    data=royalsPA,
    aes(y=reorder(Name, `OPS+`), x=`OPS+`),
    horizontal = TRUE
  ) +
    geom_lollipop(
    data=melendez,
    aes(y=reorder(Name, `OPS+`), x=`OPS+`),
    horizontal = TRUE, color="#004687"
  ) +
    geom_lollipop(
    data=massey,
    aes(y=reorder(Name, `OPS+`), x=`OPS+`),
    horizontal = TRUE, color="#BD9B60"
  ) +
  labs(title="How do the Royals stack up in Kauffman?", subtitle="OPS+ takes into account how the home ballpark plays into their stats", x="OPS+", y="Royals Players", caption="Source: Pro Baseball Reference | By Jackson Reddick") +
theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank()
    )

OPS+ is mainly focused around the home ballpark the players play in, and the MLB average is 100 points. Only six of the main stays in the Royals lineup achieved an OPS+ of 100 and I don’t know if some of the guys qualify to be honest. I highlighted Melendez and Massey because they were a pair of young guys needing to break through and just didn’t.

I love the Royals and always will, but damn they need to play like a baseball team for me to want to watch them past the third week of the season. The pitching needs to be fundamental and the offense needs to be consistent.
